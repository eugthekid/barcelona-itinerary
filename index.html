The shape of the trip

Four full days, arriving Tuesday morning and leaving Saturday morning.

Tuesday — Exploring the Gothic Quarter

Land, Aerobus to Airbnb, explore the Barcelona Cathedral and the Gothic Quarter, lunch at Boqueria, coffee at Bar Central, Ciutadella Park & El Born, & dinner at Parking Pizza Gran Via.

Morning coffee at Departure Coffee, tapas at Bar Cañete, shopping & exploring Portal de l'Àngel + Passeig de Gràcia, walking by the Sagrada Família, dinner at Casa Lolea, & watching the Magic Fountain.

Park Güell in the morning, shopping back at Portal de l'Àngel, lunch at Hideout Burger, an afternoon at Barceloneta beach, dinner at Maná 75, cheesecake at Joncake, then watching Spain vs Austria from the Airbnb.

Friday — Venturing outside of Barcelona

Train to Montserrat, monastery + basilica, Sant Miquel's Cross, Sant Joan funicular, Sant Jeroni summit, rack railway down, Mercat dels Encants, farewell dinner at El Xampanyet.

Getting there — Tuesday, 8:30 AM landing

Three ways to make the trip, fastest to cheapest. Given an early-morning arrival after an overnight flight, the taxi is the easiest call — but here's the full picture.

Option 1 · Recommended
Taxi or Uber/Cabify, direct
~20–25 min
€30–40
Official taxi rank, Terminal 1 or 2
After landing at 8:30am, we likely clear customs and reach the taxi rank by ~9–9:15am. A direct taxi to Carrer Valldonzella runs about 20–25 minutes door to door — no transfers, no stairs with luggage. Tell the driver "Carrer de Valldonzella, prop de Plaça Universitat" if needed. Note: this street is narrow and partly pedestrian, so the driver may drop us at the nearest accessible corner, a short walk from the door.
Option 2
Aerobús to Plaça de Catalunya, then walk
~40 min total
~€6
Aerobús (A1 from T1, A2 from T2)
Runs every 5–10 minutes, about a 35-minute ride to Plaça de Catalunya. From there it's a flat, easy 6-minute walk down Carrer de Pelai toward Universitat to reach Valldonzella — totally manageable with luggage since it's all on wide, level sidewalks.
Walking route

Bottom line for an 8:30am arrival: grab a taxi. We're likely jet-lagged, possibly carrying bags we don't want to haul through metro transfers, and the price difference (€30–40 vs. €6–13) is small relative to the comfort of a direct ride straight to the door.

~1:10 PM
Sant Joan Funicular up
A 7-minute ride at a 65% gradient — the steepest funicular in Spain. The glass ceiling gave views of the monastery shrinking below. We arrived at Pla de les Taràntules, 1,000m above sea level.
~1:20 PM
Sant Jeroni summit hike
We went right out of the funicular building rather than left with the crowds. About 1hr 10min to the summit at 1,236m, passing ancient hermitages, the Gorros rock formations, and a wooden footbridge. The final section was steep stone stairs.
View on map

Practical note: most shops run 10am–8:30/9pm (an hour later, to 10pm, in summer). Many independent shops close Sundays, though tourist-zone stores and Passeig de Gràcia stay open daily from mid-May to mid-September — covers our whole trip.
