Getting there — Tuesday, 8:30 AM landing

Three ways to make the trip, fastest to cheapest. Given an early-morning arrival after an overnight flight, the taxi is the easiest call — but here's the full picture.

Option 1 · Recommended
Taxi or Uber/Cabify, direct
~20–25 min
€30–40
Official taxi rank, Terminal 1 or 2
After landing at 8:30am, you'll likely clear customs and reach the taxi rank by ~9–9:15am. A direct taxi to Carrer Valldonzella runs about 20–25 minutes door to door — no transfers, no stairs with luggage. Tell the driver "Carrer de Valldonzella, prop de Plaça Universitat" if needed. Note: this street is narrow and partly pedestrian, so the driver may drop you at the nearest accessible corner, a short walk from the door.
Option 2
Aerobús to Plaça de Catalunya, then walk
~40 min total
~€6
Aerobús (A1 from T1, A2 from T2)
Runs every 5–10 minutes, about a 35-minute ride to Plaça de Catalunya. From there it's a flat, easy 6-minute walk down Carrer de Pelai toward Universitat to reach Valldonzella — totally manageable with luggage since it's all on wide, level sidewalks.
Walking route
Option 3 · Cheapest
Metro via L9 Sud → L1 (change at Torrassa)
~45–50 min total
€5.90
L9 Sud from the airport, change at Torrassa onto L1
Take L9 Sud from either terminal to Torrassa, change onto L1 (red line) toward Fondo, and get off at Universitat — a 5-minute walk from there. Workable with light luggage, but two changes and several flights of stairs/escalators make this the least appealing option right after an overnight flight.

Bottom line for an 8:30am arrival: grab a taxi. You'll likely be jet-lagged, possibly carrying bags you don't want to haul through metro transfers, and the price difference (€30–40 vs. €6–13) is small relative to the comfort of a direct ride straight to the door.

The shape of the trip

Four full days. Mornings belong to Gaudí and the old city; evenings belong to whatever's lit up — a fountain show, a fan zone, a team presentation. Saturday belongs to the airport, well before 850,000 cycling fans take over the streets.

Tue — Landing softly

Land, coffee at Lodetto, Cathedral at 12:30 (booked), Gothic Quarter wander, jet-lag nap, optional Portal de l'Àngel/vintage browse, El Born wander, dinner at Parking Pizza.

Wed — Gaudí day

Sagrada Família at opening, Park Güell at 11am (booked), Casa Batlló / Casa Milà + Passeig de Gràcia shopping, dinner in Gràcia.

Thu — Beach day

Slower morning, Barceloneta in the afternoon, Montjuïc Castle and Magic Fountain at night.

Fri — Day trip + last night

Montserrat or Sitges, then a final tapas crawl wherever you fell in love with the city.
